Who is the Youth Council Choir?

The origins of the Youth Council (YC) choir at Mary Immaculate Parish (MIP) trace back to the early 1980s. Between 1983 and 1984, Most Rev. Roberto Calara Mallari DD, the current Bishop of the Diocese of Tarlac, initiated efforts to gather the parish’s youth. By 1985, Rev. Fr. Pierino Rogliardi continued this mission by introducing the Focolare Movement to a group of young parishioners, marking the official formation of the Youth Council.

Prominent early members included Bro. Joel Abellanosa and Rene Ferrer. Over time, leadership transitioned to Yuri Alafriz and the succeeding Youth Council Primes, who played pivotal roles in expanding YC’s influence. Singing, acting, and dancing became central to YC’s activities, encapsulated in their cultural showcases called “Langit-Langitan.”

The evolution of YC took an artistic turn with the emergence of Apostles of Christ Thru Service (ACTS), a group focused on enhancing the parish’s aesthetics by displaying the monthly Words of Life, drawn from the Focolare Movement. ACTS later integrated chorister roles, solidifying their musical contributions to the parish.

Today, the Youth Council Choir remains a vital part of Mary Immaculate Parish, engaging 30+ attendees at every Sunday service and having mandatory weekly sectionals and rehearsals. Beyond music, YC fosters spiritual growth through monthly Block Rosary gatherings, one-hour vigils in the adoration chapel, sessions reflecting on the Word of Life, and yearly retreats usually in Tagaytay.

Vision and Mission

Mission:

To glorify God and honor the Blessed Virgin Mary through the power of music, nurturing a profound sense of devotion, unity, and service within our parish community.

Vision:

To serve as a guiding light of Marian spirituality within our parish, igniting souls through sacred music and cultivating a community of song and prayer guided by the virtues of Our Mother Mary and the principles of Focolare spirituality.

Activities and Contributions

  • Liturgical Services: Chorister services for weddings, funeral masses, and regular Sunday services.
  • Spiritual Formation: Monthly Word of Life reflections, Block Rosary devotions, and retreats with parish priests and seminarians.
  • Parish Outreach: Participating in feeding programs, annual sports festivals, and community-building events.
  • Music Ministry: Weekly rehearsals, monthly vigil services, and collaboration with other parish choirs.

Profound Achievements

  • Top 5 placement in the "Sound of Christmas 2015" Open Category competition held in Southmall, Las Piñas.
  • 4th place in the Villar Foundation’s chorale competition 2024, honoring San Ezekiel Moreno.
  • And many more…

Future Goals

  1. Greater involvement in choral competitions.
  2. More chorale clinics to refine their musical excellence.
  3. Increased collaborations with choirs from other parishes.
  4. Enhanced musical and spiritual Catholic Christian formations from the Diocese of Parañaque (DOP).
  5. Expanded community service within the parish.

Upcoming Milestones

  • 40th Anniversary Celebration: Scheduled for May 2025, marking four decades of YC’s legacy.
  • 2025 Parish Fiesta Hermano/Hermana Mayor: YC will make history as the first choir group to assume this role at Mary Immaculate Parish.
